# CSR Study Design Generator Prompt

## Task

Generate a CSR Study Design section from structured study design elements extracted from the CSP.

---

## Purpose

This prompt converts structured study design elements into a clear, regulatory-appropriate CSR Study Design narrative.

It should produce a coherent section that reflects the actual study design while avoiding unsupported assumptions and excessive dependence on template language.

---

## Inputs

### Structured Study Design Elements
[INSERT STRUCTURED STUDY DESIGN ELEMENTS HERE]

### Desired Level of Detail
[INSERT ONE: Expanded / Standard / Abbreviated]

### Optional Style Guidance
[INSERT CSR STYLE EXAMPLE OR NOTES HERE IF AVAILABLE]

---

## Instructions

Use the structured study design elements to draft a CSR Study Design section.

The output should:
- be coherent and self-contained
- reflect the actual study design described in the extracted elements
- use formal regulatory medical writing style
- include only elements supported by the structured extraction
- adapt the level of detail to the requested mode

### Level of Detail Guidance

- **Expanded**: include fuller design description, key operational features, treatment structure, and endpoint framing
- **Standard**: include the key design features typically expected in a CSR Study Design section
- **Abbreviated**: provide a concise study design summary covering only the essential elements

If an element is marked "Not explicitly stated", do not invent it or force it into the narrative.

---

## Rules

1. Use only the structured study design elements provided.
2. Do not introduce information not present in the extracted elements.
3. Preserve numerical values exactly.
4. Do not include placeholder text.
5. Do not mention source modules or the CSP.
6. Keep tone neutral, precise, and regulatory.

---

## Output Format

## CSR Study Design Section

[Provide the full generated narrative]

---

## Included Elements

List which extracted elements were incorporated into the final narrative.

---

## Omitted or Unclear Elements

List any elements not used because they were not explicitly stated or were too unclear to include safely.

---

## Example

This was a Phase 2, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multicenter study conducted in adult patients with [condition]. Subjects were randomized in a 1:1:1 ratio to receive one of three treatment regimens for 12 weeks. The primary endpoint was [endpoint], with secondary endpoints including [endpoint(s)].
